Department of Education Redesign

The Problem: The U.S Department of Education's site was in major need of a makeover -
the site was dated and disorganized, leaving users confused and frustrated.

The Solution: create a new navigation bar that provided a little more information to the
user, make rerouting clear to the user, and give the site an overall facelift - freshen it up at bit!

I worked with a partner to draft, execute, and consolidate the data from user interviews and user testing We completed heuristic evaluations and created our User Personas! We started with heuristic evaluations. We annotated and noted what didn’t make sense from a user perspective, colors that weren’t up to accessibility standards, and user heuristics.

Sitemaps were created after lenghty cardsorting sessions. With the help of Invision, I was able to spark up creative ideas by making a moodboard for the redesign - which would later help me create my style guide for the desktop and mobile site.

After creating the sitemap, it was time for the fun part - sketching. My sketches came to life as low-fidelity mockups. With the help of user testing, I was able to make adjustments better fitted for the user.

Which brought us to, my peronsal favorite part of the project, the high fidelity prototyping. I created my style guide and got to work bringing the site to life. After some additional testing and polishing, it was done, for now.

In Conclusion: I was able to create a better experience for the user. By reorganizing the way in which information was being presented the user is more engaged and is able to find the information they seek efficiently.
